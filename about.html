About

I'm a Mechanical Engineering student at Cornell exploring self-expression through a variety of photographic media. My personal work focuses on how modern software tools can inspire analog processes, and the creation of unsettling images in familiar spaces.

My analog images are captured on 35mm and medium-format film, as well as directly to black and white negative photopaper. I shoot with a Minolta X-370 and Hasselblad 500C inherited from both sides of my family, as well as a Nikon F-801 and Konica Autoreflex TC that I have modified and maintained extensively. I make silver gelatin prints through traditional B&W optical enlargement on Ilford Multigrade paper. Digital images are captured on Canon, Sony, and Fujifilm SLR or mirrorless systems.
