# Portraits at the Beebe Backyard Ultramarathon


In November 2025, I decided to run four miles around the same course, every hour on the hour, until I no longer could.

This was the second annual Beebe Backyard Ultra, an informal race drawing runners from across Central New York to loop Cornell's Beebe Lake as long as possible. Rules:

- The course is a single loop just short of four miles. Same loop every time.

- Runners begin the loop every hour on the hour. No early starts.

- Runners must finish each loop within 60 minutes or do not finish (DNF).

- The last runner standing wins.


I also brought a few portable studio strobes to photograph my fellow runners in the short window between finishing a lap and starting the next.

Ultramarathons are unique for the camaraderie between runners, whatever the stakes. It comes from a shared decision to do something really stupid. In the backyard format, races unfold at such slow paces, over such long times, that casual conversation is almost a requirement. Hours pass. People become friends.

The race began at 6 AM sharp with the strike of a medium-sized gong.

Molly studies biomedical engineering, runs on Cornell's Nordic Ski team, and has done an Ironman. I ran with her for almost five laps. Claire found her way into the race through Molly, starting at 9:00 AM. When I asked why they decided to run, they shrugged. Why not? Each made it over 12 laps, sitting some out along the way. They knew this would put them out of championship contention, but they were not there for a trophy.

At the start of lap eight, clouds moved in over the lake and rain started at 1:30 PM. It did not stop until morning. My legs had begun to ache, and I took a few Advil before sitting down.

After lap 11, race organizers handed out headlamps from Cornell Outdoor Education's supply. I prefer a chest-mounted setup, and it bounced considerably more while running. This visual metronome put me in a trance for most of the night, broken by occasional slips on the muddy grass around the lake and the arboretum.

For the next seven hours I ran with Lily, Andrew, and Michael. Rain-slicked course, deep fatigue. No time after any of those laps to set up for portraits. The scoreboard, a hand-drawn spreadsheet colored with crayon, had moved inside to 504 Thurston Avenue.

SRC: ![alt text](../../assets/process/beebe-backyard/scoreboard.jpeg)
Caption: The scoreboard at 9:00 PM. Image courtesy of Isla Chadsey.

I dropped out at midnight, having run approximately 65 miles, exactly 2.5 marathons as I later found out. Lily, Andrew, and Michael continued on to their 18th lap, then decided to end the race on a three-way tie.
